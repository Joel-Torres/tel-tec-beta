Giving Back To Our Communities

From the beginning, Tel-Tec has been involved in making the community a better place to live and work.


President Morgan Clayton is a former director of the Greater Bakersfield Chamber of Commerce and a chairman of the Mercy Hospital Board of Directors. He also sits on a number of regional and state-wide associations and boards.


From sponsoring local events to planning and implementing substantial programs aimed at guiding young people, Tel-Tec is devoted to serving the community and working in partnership with other businesses and organizations.

Leaders in Life

In 1998, local businessman Morgan Clayton approached the Kern County Network for Children about establishing a premier youth conference in Kern County. Planning began in the fall of 1999 with about 35 students who brainstormed and developed the first conference held in February 2000. In 20 years of the conference, approximately 40,000 students and adult chaperones have attended.


About 1,340 students have participated on the planning committee over 20 years. Selected students are invited to also serve on the Executive Committee and receive additional leadership development through the California Association of Student Councils (see www.casc.net â€“ a nationally recognized program for training youth leaders for over 60 years). Student planners, guided by adult advisors, attend monthly general planning meetings; each student also participates in one of four working subcommittees: Workshops & Speakers, Marketing & Fundraising, Resource Fair and Venue & Logistics.


The Conference has been sustained through the community outreach and the fund raising efforts of Morgan Clayton and the Kern County Superintendent of Schools and by the active involvement of youth on the planning committee.
